## Introduction

Autonomous driving systems  have emerged as a promising technology that could revolutionize the  way we travel. With the potential to enhance  road  safety improve traffic  efficiency and enable new mobility services autonomous driving has captured the  imagination of both the public and the automotive industry. However the development of autonomous driving systems is not without its challenges. In this article, we will explore the key  obstacles and complexities in developing autonomous driving  systems, aiming to  provide valuable insights into  this rapidly evolving field.

## Ensuring  Safety  and Reliability

One of  the foremost challenges in developing  autonomous driving systems is ensuring their safety and reliability. The stakes are high when it comes  to autonomous vehicles operating on public roads, and any failure or malfunction can have severe  consequences. To address this challenge researchers and engineers are constantly  exploring new methods and technologies to increase the safety  and reliability of  autonomous vehicles.

A study conducted at MIT, as reported  in a [MIT News article](https://news.mit.edu/2023/exploring-methods-increasing-safety-reliability-autonomous-vehicles-0523), highlights the potential of human supervisors in reducing barriers to deploying autonomous vehicles. By having human supervisors oversee the  autonomous driving operation, it becomes possible to intervene in critical  situations and ensure safety. This  hybrid approach, combining the capabilities of autonomous systems with the oversight of human  supervisors, shows promise in addressing safety concerns.

Developing autonomous driving  systems involves overcoming  a multitude of  technical and operational challenges. From perception and decision-making algorithms  to sensor integration and human-machine interaction there are numerous  complex components that need to work together seamlessly.

A  Reuters article titled  "Insight: Truly autonomous cars may be impossible without helpful human touch" delves into the challenges faced by autonomous vehicle startups in developing truly self-driving cars. The  article accessible [here](https://www.reuters.com/technology/truly-autonomous-cars-may-be-impossible-without-helpful-human-touch-2022-09-12/), highlights the importance of human intervention and the limitations of current autonomous driving technology.
